Meet the Xbox Content and Design Team

Scott Selfon (Audio Content Consultant, Xbox Content and Design Team)

Scott has been active in music composition since the age of 8, and his first (student) film score was completed at the age of 13. He has composed for a wide range of media, including film and television, PC titles and miscellaneous multimedia. Among his most recent projects are: Seattle International Film Festival's Fly Filmmaking program, "Over Your Head" (independent short), "Robit" for SIGGRAPH, collaborative work on VR-1 Crossroads (the first online title to support DirectMusic), and sample content for the DirectX SDK. Scott has also created a number of live performance works for acoustic and electronic instruments.

Scott is the artistic director, arranger, and (grudging) baritone of an a cappella group at the University of Washington, as well as the principal violist in the Sammamish Symphony, the Puget Sound Symphony, and the Microtones String Quartet.

Prior to joining the Xbox team, Scott served as the test lead for DirectMusic Producer and DirectX Audio Scripting. Previously, he was a Program Manager on the MSN Creative Audio team, which focused on the sonification of web content using interactive soundtracks.

Scott is a graduate of the University of Southern California, where he obtained dual degrees in Music Composition (film scoring emphasis) and Computer Engineering/Computer Science.
