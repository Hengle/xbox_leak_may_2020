Common Models for User Interfaces

In your game, it is frequently necessary to describe how the various buttons, thumbsticks, and directional pad of the Xbox Controller are utilized. For example, you may have a screen similar to the following:



Here are some other common scenarios:

  • You want to demonstrate how your new controller plugs into the Xbox. In your documenation, you include a picture of the Xbox and the new controllers being plugged in.
  • You want to tell players of your game that they have saved too many games on their memory unit. A 3-D model of the Xbox Memory Unit (MU) pops up with a message telling the players that they need to save their games on a different MU.

To help developers with these kinds of scenarios, we have included the models below. For each model, there is a 3D-Studio Max file and its textures, which have been zipped together. To download the models, click on the links or pictures below.
